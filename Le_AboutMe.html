When I was little, I was always interested in how a computer works internally. This interest has always stuck with me for the rest of my years in school, it led me to where I am now. With the interest I have in computers I hope to create or contribute to something that will benefit people from all over the world. I plan to pursue a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science to further my knowledge about computers, I will then apply for a job in a software company to get a consistent income. Throughout these years I will keep creating applications that will either enhance my knowledge or will be beneficial to people around the world.

I excel in a group environment through my open-mindedness and dedication to a cause. When a group is first formed together, people are usually defensive and not willing to open up with others. I combat this by being the first one to talk and by asking others to share things about themselves, I try to create a strong foundation for the group to excel.

I had always enjoyed doing math but when I first moved to a higher math course, my view of math changed. I felt like I was being bombarded with material that I couldn't understand. To make matters worse, I didn't have someone to support me when I was struggling. No matter how hard I tried, the test would constantly get harder and updated with new material. In the end, I had managed to pass the course and continue on an advanced math path. Throughout my initial struggle, I had developed new study habits that I would still use today. Additionally, it taught me that the early stages of mastery is often grueling but is essential to the growth of that skill.

To summarize, my goal is to be able to impact the world in a meaningful way through my creations. I am valuable in a group environment and that I have learned to persevere in times of struggle.
